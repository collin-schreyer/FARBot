Heading: 15.305 Proposal evaluation.
Text: (a) (a)
                  
                  
						            Proposal evaluation is an assessment of the proposal and the offeror’s ability to perform the prospective contract successfully. An agency shall evaluate competitive proposals and then assess their relative qualities solely on the factors and subfactors specified in the solicitation. Evaluations may be conducted using any rating method or combination of methods, including color or adjectival ratings, numerical weights, and ordinal rankings. The relative strengths, deficiencies, significant weaknesses, and risks supporting proposal evaluation shall be documented in the contract file.
(b) (b)
                  
                  
						            The source selection authority may reject all proposals received in response to a solicitation, if doing so is in the best interest of the Government.
(c) (c)
                  
                  
						            For restrictions on the use of support contractor personnel in proposal evaluation, see 37.203(d).
